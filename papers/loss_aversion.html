Explaining Domain-Specific Risk-Taking with Loss Aversion.

In progress

In this study, we will directly examine the associations between domain specific risk taking and loss aversion under the framework of prospect theory. From the risky decision making literature, attempts that tried to assess individualâ€™s risk preference basically fall into one of the two categories: 1. measuring risk preference as a general tendency toward risk taking, or 2.measuring risk preference as a domain-specific construct. The conceptualization and measurement of risk preference has been a major focus of both sides, but little consensus has been achieved. In this paper, we hypothesize that loss aversion could be seen as a trans-situational measure for risk preference and it could help explains individual risk taking behavior across domains.
