Breaking Bad: Predicting Corrupt Behavior in a Bribery Game Experiment

In progress

During the past two decades, the phenomenon of corruption has attracted more and more attentions from economists. Most past studies have focused on the effect of institutions, yet very few studies have been conducted to investigate the effect of individual difference on corruptive behavior. In the current study, we examine how individual differences in personality traits could affect corruptive behavior in a controlled laboratory experiment.
