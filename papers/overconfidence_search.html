Overconfidence and Self-selection in a Sequential Decision-Making Task.

In progress

In the paper, we try to demonstrate how overconfidence may affect people’s decision in complex tasks. In specific, we first break the notion of overconfidence into three manifestations: above-average bias, illusion of control, and mis-calibration. We then look at how each manifestation influences the decision maker’s performance when solving an optimal stopping problem. We propose and experimentally test the hypothesis that the above-average bias often hurt the decision maker by impeding them from accepting advantageous default setting.
