The Premium for being a Nice Person: Agreeableness and the Gender Wage Gap among Recent Graduates in China.

Abstract

Using a unique dataset from China, we investigate the effect of agreeableness, an important personality trait, on the respondents’ starting salaries. We propose and empirically test the hypothesis that keeping all other things equal, disagreeable women face a sanction for violating the ascribed gender stereotypes. Results from our analyses indicate that agreeableness has negligible effect on men’s starting salaries, yet it dramatically impacts women’s starting salaries. Less-agreeable women earn less than the others. This effect is highly robust to changes in model specifications. Our result suggests the importance of investigating the gender wage gap from an individual-culture dynamic perspective. Personality traits and cultural stereotypes jointly impact labor market outcomes.
