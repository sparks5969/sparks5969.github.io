Research Statement

My main research interest is behavioral and experimental economics. A central focus of my work is to bridge economics and psychology, use lab experiments, game theory, and quantitative method such as machine learning to investigate questions that are of fundamental importance in social science research: why people make decisions in varied ways and how different social outcomes are achieved in interactive environments.
